Trope offers a dynamic, non-passive viewing experience for users. This web experience was built with the goal of giving both producer and audience more control in their ability to leverage the Internet to share their knowledge.
>Trope takes a video file and converts it into a series >of image files, frame by frame. >As the user >scrolls, the length of the scroll is directly matched to a >frame in the video. So, as the user scrolls down, the frames >move forward, and as the user scrolls back up, the frames >move backwards.
>Currently the most common interaction when viewing >media, like videos or gifs, allows little to no control. A gif >is very passive, while a video gives you the ability to play, >stop, or pause. --- >__With Trope you must >actively scroll to move the frames forward or backward.__ ---
_This interaction gives the user complete control over how they watch the content, at what part to start, how fast or slow they scroll through, or which parts they want to re-watch._
__Giving a more experiential control to the user, providing much more agency and thus a stronger ability to watch the content how they want to watch it.__

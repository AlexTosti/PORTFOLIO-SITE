Little Boxes was a real time, digitally interactive set of time capsules, in the form of little wooden boxes. Presenting intertwined personal histories of participants, Little Boxes focused on exploring different ways we can experience and sort our personal online data.

Upon logging onto our Facebook application guests had the choice to choose a date on our time machine. Scattered around the room, the tiny screens nestled in wooden boxes of all sizes switch to that date, displaying videos, comments, news articles, music, photographs and status updates. Creating a complete blend of personal and communal history’s as the guests explore the boxes, seeing their own memories as well as the others in the room combined with general moments of note from that time.

Little Boxes was an interactive installation as a part of Sydney University’s Organized Cacophony exhibit held in The Rocks, Sydney.

the boxes

We wanted to create a tactile experience that felt personal to the viewer, so the little boxes themselves, were hand made and designed to hold a variety of different devices, laptops, iPhones and iPads.

Each box contains a small screen so each of the boxes could be held by hand and brought up to the face for viewing, to create a more intimate and exploratory viewing experience.

the Data / System

The overall system was created using the facebook api, instagram api, youtube api, as well as our own hand picked database, consisting of site specific news headlines and points of interest for specific dates. Each of the devices were running as a client side program which our center console communicated with via websockets.
